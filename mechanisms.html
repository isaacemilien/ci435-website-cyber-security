Mechanisms

Cyber security mechanisms are precausions put in place to defend against theats to security

Firewall

Traffic

A firewall is a tool that monitors traffic going into and out of a computer or network and either allows the traffic to pass through or blocks it.

Blocking

The decision to allow or block is based on rules known as the firewall policy. For example, some programs, such as email clients and web browsers, have legitimate cause to send a transmission. These programs are known, and the firewall policy allows their communications. However, any transmissions not sent from or to known - and allowed - sources are blocked.

Products

Firewalls can be hardware-based or software-based. Hardware firewalls tend to be more expensive but are more effective.

Antivirus

Uses

An antivirus program detects and removes viruses and other malicious software from your computer or laptop.

Harm

Malicious software (malware) is code that can harm your computers and laptops and their data. Your devices can become infected by inadvertently downloading malware in an attachment linked to a dubious email, hidden on a USB drive, or simply visiting a dodgy website.

Once it's on your computer or laptop, malware can steal your data, encrypt it so you can't access it, or even erase it. For this reason, it's vital that you always use antivirus software and keep it up to date to protect your data and devices.

Protection

Firewalls can be hardware-based or software-based. Hardware firewalls tend to be more expensive but are more effective.

Antivirus software scans files on a computer and compares specific bits of code against a database of known malware. It will then quarantine the file and delete malicious code to prevent malware from causing damage to your device. Modern antivirus products update themselves automatically to protect against the latest viruses and other types of malware. Still, problems arise when newer viruses are created, as the longer the gap between updates, the less protection it can offer.
